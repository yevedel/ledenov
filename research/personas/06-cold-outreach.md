# Cold outreach — 4 templates (one per persona)

The pattern the research validates, used in every template:
**Name the specific scar → offer a de-risked first step → prove the design+front-end combo → soft CTA = a teardown/trial, never "let's talk about a retainer."**

Keep it short. Lead with *their* problem, not your bio. The CTA is always low-commitment (the trial is the front door; the relationship is earned after).

---

## Persona 1 — The Embarrassed Builder (technical founder)
**Where:** HN (Show HN / launches), founder Discords/Slacks, GitHub-adjacent communities, product launches with rough UI.
**Trigger to reach out:** they just shipped/launched something functional but visually rough.

> **Subject:** your product works — it just doesn't look like it (yet)
>
> Hi [name] — saw [product]. The logic is clearly solid; the UI is the thing holding back how trustworthy it feels (totally normal — devs make functional stuff that looks average, that's not your job).
>
> I'm a senior product designer who also writes front-end. I'd redesign your [core flow] and hand it off as **build-ready React/Tailwind** — components and tokens, so your team ships it instead of reverse-engineering a Figma file. And I propose the calls and push back, so you're not stuck art-directing a designer.
>
> Want a free 5-minute teardown of your [signup/dashboard]? No call, I'll just send notes + a Loom.

**Why:** removes design *and* management load; "designer who codes"; CTA is zero-commitment.

## Persona 2 — The Stranded Non-Technical Founder
**Where:** Indie Hackers, r/startups / r/SaaS posts asking "how do I find a good designer/dev," founders who just posted about a freelancer who burned them.
**Trigger:** a post venting about ghosting / money-pit / a stalled build.

> **Subject:** so you don't get burned twice
>
> Hi [name] — saw your post about [the freelancer who ghosted / the build that stalled]. That pattern is brutally common, and it's usually about *how* someone works, not talent.
>
> I'm a senior designer + front-end dev who ships founders' products end-to-end. How I work so it doesn't happen again: **you own every file and the IP, always**; scope and price set on one call, **no surprise invoices**; and I don't go dark — I stay through implementation.
>
> Easiest way to test me with near-zero risk: I'll do a small first piece (one screen/flow) so you can see the work and how I communicate before committing to anything. Happy to share a shipped product you can click and a client you can talk to first. Worth a quick look?

**Why:** reliability + ownership + proof (what this buyer says converts), and a small paid trial = their exact de-risking behavior.

## Persona 3 — The Funded Operator (Series A–B)
**Where:** warm intros first (ask your network / their investors); else LinkedIn, founders posting about rebrands/fundraises/enterprise pushes.
**Trigger:** fragmenting brand across freelancers, a raise/repositioning, or an agency relationship going sideways.

> **Subject:** one accountable line for product, site & brand
>
> Hi [name] — congrats on [raise / launch]. At your stage design usually fragments: a few capable freelancers, an agency, no one embedded enough to own how it all adds up — so the brand drifts and velocity leaks to context-loss.
>
> I work as **one accountable senior partner across product, site, and brand** — embedded in your Slack and standups, shipping weekly, no account managers and no junior-swap after you sign. You talk to the person doing the work. And it's not a bus-factor risk: documented build-ready files, a vetted backup on call, clean handover if you scale to an in-house team.
>
> Could start with a 2-week sprint so it's low-commitment. Want me to send a short read on your current product + brand consistency first?

**Why:** partner/continuity/accountability frame, bus-factor pre-empt, sprint on-ramp, status-appropriate. No "freelancer," no bargain pricing.

## Persona 4 — The Design-Literate Repeat Buyer
**Where:** design-literate founder networks, X/IH where they post opinions on craft, people who've written about their last designer.
**Trigger:** starting something new, or publicly valuing taste/judgment.

> **Subject:** a co-pilot, not a pixel-pusher
>
> Hi [name] — you clearly care about craft, so I'll skip the portfolio dump. Execution is commoditizing fast; taste, judgment, and a point of view aren't — that's what I bring, and I'll push back when I think you're wrong.
>
> I'm a senior product designer + front-end dev, AI-accelerated but senior-directed (the tools plus the judgment they can't replace). Here's the scrappy in-progress thinking behind a product I shipped [link] — decisions and the alternatives I rejected, not a glossy case study.
>
> If you're looking for a design partner who stays and scales with you, worth a conversation?

**Why:** leads with point-of-view and decision-making (what this buyer screens for), signals AI fluency, frames the *relationship* not a deliverable.

---

## Universal rules
- **Subject + first line name the scar**, not you. If they don't see their own problem in line one, you're deleted.
- **CTA is always the small step** (teardown / one flow / sprint). "Let's discuss a retainer" is the #1 way to lose every persona — the partnership is back-doored through a successful trial.
- **Always surface the design+front-end combo** — it's the differentiator buyers actively hunt for and the thing that kills their rework fear.
- **Proof on request, not upfront:** offer "a shipped product you can click + a client you can call" — references beat case studies for the burned buyers (P2/P3).
- **Match the scar to the persona:** ghosting/ownership (P2), fragmentation/bait-switch (P3), art-directing/handoff (P1), order-takers/taste (P4).
