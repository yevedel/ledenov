## Project Catalog — 3 cards = the conversion ladder

### Card 1 — Entry / de-risk (Persona 2 & 1 first touch)
**Title:** `UX teardown + redesign plan for your product`
**Frame:** Send your product URL; get a senior teardown of where users drop off + a prioritized fix plan (and a Loom walkthrough). No call required.
**Price:** low (e.g. starter tier ~$150–300; or run the free async version off-platform and use this as the paid, deeper one).
**Why it works:** matches the universal "pay-them-a-small-thing-to-vet-them" behavior; the free/cheap teardown is the front door the research says converts. CTA out of it = Card 2.

### Card 2 — The trial that leaves a foundation (the workhorse — all personas)
**Title:** `Core flow redesigned + build-ready handoff (React/Tailwind)`
**Frame:** One core flow (onboarding, dashboard, signup, key screen set) redesigned end-to-end and handed off as build-ready components + tokens, so your team ships it without rework. Fixed scope, fixed price.
**Tiers:** 
- *Basic* — 1 flow, Figma + handoff notes.
- *Standard* — 1 flow + build-ready React/Tailwind components + Loom handoff.
- *Premium* — multi-screen set + design system primitives + front-end handoff.
**Price:** from ~$500 (Basic) scaling up.
**Why it works:** this is the "foundation from day one, not an audition" trial. It proves the design+dev combo and the no-rework promise in one paid step. Lead conversion artifact for Personas 1 & 2.

### Card 3 — The relationship (Persona 3 & 4)
**Title:** `Embedded design partner — 2-week sprint (ongoing optional)`
**Frame:** I embed as your senior design partner across product, site, and brand — in your Slack and standups, shipping weekly. Start with a 2-week sprint; continue ongoing if it's working. One accountable senior person, no agency middlemen, no junior swap.
**Price:** sprint priced like a real engagement (anchor to ~$1,000/week); ongoing = weekly retainer.
**Why it works:** gives funded/sophisticated buyers the *partner* frame and a low-commitment on-ramp (sprint → ongoing), while pricing senior. Include the bus-factor line (documented files, backup on call, clean handover) to neutralize the "solo = risk" objection.

## Proposal tactics (per persona, when replying to jobs)
- **Long-term/"ongoing" posts → Persona 3/4:** open with the partner frame + "you talk to the person doing the work, no bait-and-switch." Show a decision-narrative, not glossy shots.
- **MVP/"need a designer to build X" → Persona 2:** lead with reliability + ownership ("you own every file," references), offer to start with a small paid piece.
- **"Dev team needs a designer" / technical → Persona 1:** lead with build-ready handoff + "I push back so you don't art-direct me."
- **Always:** the CTA is the teardown or a small trial — never "let's discuss a retainer."
