## Project Catalog — the ladder, repriced so the senior offer leads

> Test finding: the cheap $150–500 anchors *repel the funded operator* (read as junior next to a $2M badge). So **lead with the embedded engagement; frame the teardown explicitly as a free sample, not the lead price.** Card 2 has no standalone hook — present it as the natural upgrade after the teardown, not a front-door offer.

### Card A (lead for high-ACV) — the relationship — Persona 3 & 4
**Title:** `Embedded design partner — start with a 2-week sprint (ongoing optional)`
**Frame:** I embed as your senior design partner across product, site and brand — in your Slack and standups, shipping weekly. Principal-only: you work with me, never a junior swap or an account manager. Start with a 2-week sprint; continue ongoing if it's working. Bus-factor covered: documented build-ready files, vetted backup on call, clean handover.
**Price:** **from $1,000/week, principal-only.** (Lead with this — it signals senior, not transactional.)

### Card B (the free front door — labeled as a sample) — pulls 3 of 4
**Title:** `Free no-call UX teardown of your product`
**Frame:** Send your product URL → get a written senior teardown of where users drop off, a prioritized fix plan, and a 60-sec Loom. No call, no cost, no strings. It's a sample of how I think, on *your* product.
**Price:** **Free** (resolve the old free-vs-$150 contradiction — the entry is free).

### Card C (the upgrade after the sample) — the trial that leaves a foundation
**Title:** `Core flow redesigned + build-ready React/Tailwind handoff`
**Frame:** After the teardown, the obvious next step: one core flow (onboarding, dashboard, signup) redesigned end-to-end and handed off as **build-ready components + tokens** your team merges without rework. Fixed scope, fixed price.
**Tiers:** Basic (Figma + handoff notes) / Standard (+ React/Tailwind components + Loom) / Premium (multi-screen + design-system primitives).
**Price:** from ~$500. Position as "the foundation," not a bargain.

## Proposal tactics (per persona, when replying to jobs)
- **Long-term/"ongoing" posts → Persona 3/4:** open with the partner frame + "you talk to the principal, no junior swap." Show a decision-narrative, not glossy shots.
- **MVP/"need a designer to build X" → Persona 2:** lead with reliability + ownership ("you own every file," a named reference), offer to start with a small paid piece.
- **"Dev team needs a designer" / technical → Persona 1:** lead with "I write the components your engineers merge" + the Loom artifact + "I push back so you don't art-direct me."
- **Always:** CTA = the free teardown or a scoped first piece — never "let's discuss a retainer."
