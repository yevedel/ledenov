# Persona 2 — The Stranded Non-Technical Founder

> **One-liner:** A non-technical founder for whom the designer/dev *is* how the product exists at all — already burned once patching together freelancers, now terrified of getting ripped off again but with no way to judge craft.

*Evidence base: very strong (the richest, most visceral raw scar-tissue verbatims came from this buyer). Highest-anxiety persona.*

---

## Stage & context
- **Funding/stage:** Pre-seed to Seed; frequently bootstrapped or pre-raise, runway measured in months.
- **Team:** Solo or founder + 1. **Cannot build.** Has a sharp idea and a deadline, not a product.
- **What they've built:** A pile of docs, maybe a no-code attempt (Webflow/Bubble) that hit a wall, and/or a half-finished freelancer build. Nothing they're proud to show.
- **On fire right now:** They need a *credible, shippable* MVP — to launch, to get first users, or to raise — and the last attempt to get there failed expensively. Every dollar and week feels existential.

## Jobs to be done
- **Functional:** Get an actual usable product/MVP designed and shipped; something credible enough to put in front of users or investors; one person who **owns the outcome** and can coordinate or hand to development.
- **Emotional:** Not get burned again. Not be left helpless or locked out. Feel *safe* handing over their baby. Look legitimate before they actually are.

## The hiring trigger
A **failed first attempt** + a deadline. "I tried Webflow, dabbled in Bubble, even hired a freelancer on Upwork… either the tools had limits or the freelancers weren't as solid as their profiles looked." The moment they accept they need a real senior person, not another cheap gamble — usually right after a launch or fundraise date appears on the calendar.

## "Long-term" in their head
- **What it looks like:** ONE reliable person who *stays and owns it*, not a rotating cast they re-brief and re-hire. "If you treat it like a partnership rather than a hand-off, you get way better results."
- **Commits when:** the person is *reliable, communicative, and proactive* — sets expectations, shares updates, doesn't go dark; weekly cadence so they can "gauge if the person can actually produce results over time rather than investing so much upfront and crossing your fingers."
- **Churns when:** ghosting, missed deadlines, surprise upcharges, withheld source files, the "money pit."

## Scar tissue → objections
- "A **$25K WordPress pit**." / "$12k… **total frauds**, classic shiny promises, zero results." / "$36k over six months… **either in house or nothing**."
- "Great at first but **ghosted once it came to integration** — and I didn't have access to the repository."
- "**The freelancer hadn't sent the source files.**"
- **Can't vet craft** — the "a terrible freelancer can still have a 4.9 rating" blind spot bites them hardest.
- → **Objections:** "How do I know you won't take my money and vanish like the last one?" · "How do I even tell if you're good?" · "Will I actually own what I paid for?"

## Decision criteria & hard deal-breakers
- **Wants:** reliability and communication *above raw talent*; ownership of outcome; clear scope and price; proof it actually shipped; references they can talk to; files/IP in their name.
- **Deal-breakers:** vague scope, surprise "that costs extra," going dark, pushing off-platform immediately, no verifiable shipped work, no references, big upfront commitment with nothing to show.

## Where they look / how they vet
- **Where:** **referrals heavily** (they can't judge craft themselves), Upwork (leans on Top Rated, total spend, star ratings, review count as proxies), founder communities.
- **Vets by:** a **small paid trial task** before revealing the full vision; **client references over case studies**; milestone payments; "vetting is key — detailed case studies, testimonials, clear scope documents upfront."

## Messaging that LANDS vs that REPELS
- **Lands:** "**You own every file and all the IP — always.**" · "Scoped on a short call, fixed number, **no surprise invoices**." · "**I don't disappear at handoff** — I stay through implementation." · "Start with one small paid piece before you commit to anything." · "Here's a real shipped product you can click and a client you can call." (Reliability and proof beat talent claims for this buyer.)
- **Repels:** jargon, "trust me," vague/hourly pricing, requiring a big upfront commitment, portfolios with no evidence anything *shipped*, anything that pattern-matches to the person who burned them.
