# Outreach adapted by channel

Same logic as `06-cold-outreach.md` (name the scar → de-risked first step → design+dev proof → small CTA), retuned for the constraints of each channel. The 4 persona cores plug into any channel using these rules.

---

## LinkedIn

**Constraints:** connection-request note ≤ ~300 chars and *no links*; first DM after connect should be short and human; LinkedIn punishes anything that reads like a pitch template. Best for **Persona 3 (Funded Operator)** and **Persona 4 (Design-Literate)** — they live here and respond to peer-tone.

**Rules:** no subject line; lead with one specific observation about *them/their company*; no link in the connection note (save it for the reply); CTA = a question, not a calendar link.

**Connection request note (Persona 3):**
> Hi [name] — saw [company] just [raised / launched X]. I work as an embedded senior design partner (product + site + brand) for founders at exactly this stage. Not pitching — would just like to connect.

**First DM after they accept (Persona 3):**
> Thanks for connecting. Quick context in case it's ever useful: at your stage design usually fragments across a few freelancers/an agency and the brand drifts because no one's embedded enough to own the whole. I'm one accountable senior partner for product, site and brand — and a designer who also writes front-end, so handoff doesn't bottleneck eng. If it's ever relevant, happy to send a short read on your current product + brand consistency. No agenda.

**Connection note (Persona 4):**
> Hi [name] — liked your take on [their post about craft/taste/product]. I'm a senior product designer + front-end dev who cares about the same things. Would like to connect.

**First DM (Persona 4):**
> Appreciate the connect. I'll skip the portfolio dump — execution's commoditizing, taste and judgment aren't, and that's what I optimize for (I push back when I think a call's wrong). If you ever want a design partner who stays and scales with you, here's the scrappy thinking behind something I shipped: [link]. Decisions + the alternatives I rejected, not a glossy case study.

## Email

**Constraints:** you get a subject line and can include links; slightly more room, but the first 1.5 lines still decide everything (preview pane). Best for **Persona 1 (Embarrassed Builder)** and **Persona 2 (Stranded Non-Technical)** — found via their product/launch/posts.

**Rules:** subject names the scar; first line references their actual product; one link max above the fold; CTA = free teardown / small first piece; signature has proof (Top Rated, a clickable case).

**Persona 1:**
> **Subject:** [product]'s UI is underselling how good the product is
>
> Hi [name] — tried [product] after [saw it on HN / your launch]. The logic is clearly strong; the interface is the part making it feel less trustworthy than it is (normal — that's not a dev's job).
>
> I'm a senior product designer who also writes front-end. I'd redesign your [core flow] and hand it off as **build-ready React/Tailwind** so your team ships it instead of reverse-engineering a Figma file — and I propose the calls, so you're not art-directing me.
>
> Want a free 5-min teardown of your [signup/dashboard]? No call — I'll send notes + a Loom.
> [Name] · Top Rated Plus, 10+ yrs · [one clickable case]

**Persona 2:**
> **Subject:** so the next designer doesn't ghost you
>
> Hi [name] — saw your post about [the build that stalled / freelancer who vanished]. Brutally common, and it's about *how* someone works, not talent.
>
> I'm a senior designer + front-end dev who ships founders' products end-to-end. So it doesn't happen again: **you own every file and the IP**, scope + price set on one call (no surprise invoices), and I stay through implementation.
>
> Lowest-risk way to test me: a small first piece — one screen/flow — before you commit to anything. I can share a shipped product you can click and a client you can call first. Worth a look?
> [Name] · Top Rated Plus · [clickable case] · [reference available]

## Upwork (proposal to a job post)

**Constraints:** you're replying to *their* posted job; no off-platform contact in the first message; they're comparing 10–50 proposals and skim the first 2 lines; CTA routes to a trial / your Project Catalog, not "let's hop on a call elsewhere." Upwork buyers vet on JSS, Top Rated, reviews, total spend — so a *specific, scoped* opener beats a bio.

**Rules:** first line proves you read the post (reference a specific detail); line 2 = the relevant scar/outcome; offer a **small paid trial / scoped first deliverable**; surface the design+front-end combo; end with one concrete question about *their* product. No generic "I'm passionate about design."

**Reply to an "ongoing / long-term designer" post (Persona 3):**
> You wrote you want someone *ongoing*, not a one-off — that's exactly how I work. I'm one senior partner for product, site and brand (and I write front-end, so handoff doesn't stall your devs). You'd talk to me, not an account manager or a junior after signing.
> Suggestion to de-risk it: start with a 2-week sprint on [the specific thing in their post]; continue if it's working. Quick Q — is [X from their post] the most urgent surface, or is it [Y]?

**Reply to a "need a designer to build our MVP" post (Persona 2):**
> Saw you need [their MVP] designed and shipped — and that [detail from post]. I do exactly this end-to-end, and I hand off build-ready (React/Tailwind) so it actually gets built, not stuck in Figma.
> How I keep it safe for you: you own all files/IP, fixed scope from a call, no surprise invoices. We could start with just [one core flow] so you can see the work before committing. What's the launch date you're working toward?

**Reply to a "dev team needs design help" post (Persona 1):**
> You've got the engineering — you need the design layer that ships *into* it. I design + write front-end, so you get build-ready components, not a Figma file your team has to reverse-engineer. I also propose the direction, so I'm not extra management overhead.
> Want me to start with [the one flow they mentioned] as a scoped first piece? And — what's your stack (React/Next/Tailwind)?

## Cross-channel rules
- **Match channel to persona:** LinkedIn → P3/P4 (peer tone, no links in note); Email → P1/P2 (found via product/post); Upwork proposal → whoever posted, reference their post.
- **First line is theirs, not yours**, on every channel.
- **CTA is always the small step** (teardown / one flow / 2-week sprint). Never lead with "retainer."
- **Design+front-end combo** appears in every message — it's the differentiator and the rework-fear killer.
- **Proof by reference on request** (clickable case + a client to call) beats front-loading a portfolio, especially for P2/P3.
