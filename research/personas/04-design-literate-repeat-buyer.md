# Persona 4 — The Design-Literate Repeat Buyer

> **One-liner:** A founder who *gets* design — second-time founder, ex-designer/PM, or design-driven by conviction — and hires an embedded partner *on purpose, from day one*. Buys the relationship, not a deliverable; screens hard for taste, judgment, and the ability to push back.

*Evidence base: solid (HN + founder essays + the "execution is cheap, taste is everything" thread). The clearest embodiment of the retention thesis: they want to find a great person and KEEP them.*

---

## Stage & context
- **Funding/stage:** Spans Seed to Series B — defined by *mindset*, not stage. Often a repeat founder or someone with design/product chops.
- **Team:** Any size, but design is treated as strategic from the start ("over 40% of companies hired a designer in their first three hires or had a designer co-founder").
- **What they've built:** Usually further along and more intentional; they already know what "good" looks like and have a point of view on their product.
- **On fire right now:** They want to *move fast with a partner who has taste and judgment* and not babysit. Possibly they previously had a great designer who churned out (boredom/maintenance) or watched fragmentation kill momentum — so they're optimizing for a relationship that *lasts*.

## Jobs to be done
- **Functional:** A senior partner with **taste** who self-directs, brings ideas, pushes back, and owns outcomes. In an AI-commodity world, "execution is cheap; the limiting factor is the quality of ideas and creative direction."
- **Emotional:** A genuine creative *peer / co-pilot*. The identity of having "my design partner." And — distinctively — the desire to *keep* that person: retention is their explicit goal, not an accident.

## The hiring trigger
**Proactive, not reactive.** They go looking when they *start* something or when they decide commodity production isn't enough — "I need judgment and taste, not a pixel-pusher." Less "the UI is on fire," more "I want the right partner in place before it matters."

## "Long-term" in their head
- **What it looks like:** This is their frame from day one — they're hiring a relationship. A co-pilot through pivots, not "a passenger." Someone who could "scale with the company and become the first design leader."
- **Commits when:** taste + autonomy + strategic pushback + reliability are all present; the person keeps generating fresh, high-stakes thinking.
- **Churns when:** the person turns into an *order-taker* with no opinions, runs out of taste/ideas, can't keep pace, or the work goes stale into "maintenance" (the documented reason great embedded designers leave).

## Scar tissue → objections
- Previously had a strong designer **churn out once it became maintenance** — or hired "capable" people who were pure executors and added up to nothing.
- Knows the "Figma jockey" trap intimately and screens to avoid it.
- → **Objections:** "Will you actually have a point of view, or just do what I say?" · "Are you a builder with taste, or a tool operator?" · "Will you still be interesting to work with in six months?"

## Decision criteria & hard deal-breakers
- **Wants:** demonstrable taste and judgment; initiative; opinionated design ("we validate ideas driven by taste, not the other way around"); AI/tooling fluency; the ability to push back and be right.
- **Deal-breakers:** order-taker positioning; **no point of view**; "tell me what you want and I'll build it"; indifference to modern/AI tooling ("if a designer shows zero interest in prototyping with AI, that's a red flag"); aesthetics over strategy; can't operate independently.

## Where they look / how they vet
- **Where:** their **network and peer referrals**; reverse-engineering products they admire; design-community reputation.
- **Vets by:** a **Figma walkthrough read for decision-making** and "the scrappy in-progress work you're still proud of" (not the polished case study); questions that test for partner behavior vs. order-taker ("walk me through a time a client pushed back and you held your ground").

## Messaging that LANDS vs that REPELS
- **Lands:** "**Senior judgment and taste — AI-accelerated, self-directed.**" · "I bring a point of view and push back; you get a co-pilot, not a passenger." · "A design partner who *stays* and scales with you." · Show *decision-making and opinions*, not deliverables. Lead with thinking, not pixels.
- **Repels:** order-taker language ("just tell me what you need"), generic process, no point of view, glossy case studies with no rationale, AI-skepticism. This buyer is repelled by anything that smells like a vendor executing tickets.
