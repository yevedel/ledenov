# Persona 3 — The Funded Operator

> **One-liner:** A funded, time-poor founder whose design need has become *continuous* across product, site, and brand — tired of fragmentation from a rotating cast of freelancers/agencies, but not ready to hire a Head of Design. Wants a senior *partner*, not a vendor.

*Evidence base: solid but leans on HN + founder essays (First Round, Lenny, Designer Fund) and the agency-churn pattern rather than raw Reddit war stories — flagged honestly. This is the client's on-strategy core target ("before they can afford a full team").*

---

## Stage & context
- **Funding/stage:** Series A to Series B. Budget exists; time does not.
- **Team:** 10–50+, engineering-heavy, design under-resourced (zero or one overstretched designer).
- **What they've built:** A real product with traction. Now product + marketing site + brand + pitch decks + design system all need ongoing design, and they're being made inconsistently by different hands.
- **On fire right now:** **Fragmentation** — "nobody embedded enough to care what it all added up to." Brand looks incoherent to enterprise buyers and the board; velocity is draining to context-loss; a fundraise or enterprise push is raising the credibility bar. A full-time senior/Head of Design feels premature or slow to hire.

## Jobs to be done
- **Functional:** One accountable senior partner who carries **product + site + brand** with continuity and consistent quality at founder speed; offload design leadership without a full-time exec hire.
- **Emotional (status layer):** Wants a *peer* — "my design partner," not "my designer." Wants to look credible to the board and enterprise customers. Wants to stop managing vendors and middlemen.

## The hiring trigger
A **repositioning or fundraise moment** colliding with **vendor fatigue**: they've "cycled through capable freelancers, each producing quality work, but collectively creating a fragmented brand," or got the **agency bait-and-switch** ("sales call with a senior… work done by juniors"). The trigger is the realization that they need *continuity and ownership*, which neither freelancers nor agencies gave them.

## "Long-term" in their head
- **What it looks like:** Embedded — in Slack, in standups, in roadmap — building context "almost as fast as a full-time hire," operating as a **co-creator with a seat at the strategy table**.
- **Commits when:** the partner *owns outcomes* (not hours), pushes back strategically, keeps brand coherent across surfaces, and can credibly "scale with the company and become the first design leader later."
- **Churns when:** it degrades to junior-quality or maintenance-only, reintroduces agency overhead/middlemen, or can't operate at their altitude.

## Scar tissue → objections
- **Agency junior bait-and-switch**; **fragmented brand** from too many freelancers; slow, process-heavy retainers where "you're buying hours, not outcomes"; "pretty reports while the bottom line doesn't move."
- → **Objections:** "Can one person actually cover product *and* brand *and* site at our scale?" · "Is this senior, or will I get junior work after I sign?" · "Is a solo person a bottleneck / bus-factor risk?"

## Decision criteria & hard deal-breakers
- **Wants:** demonstrable senior judgment; cross-functional breadth (product + brand + marketing) from *one accountable person*; outcome ownership; a direct line to the person doing the work; a continuity/bus-factor answer.
- **Deal-breakers:** bait-and-switch ("who actually executes?"); account managers/middlemen; process theater; cheap pricing that signals junior; aesthetics-over-business; inability to talk to engineers/board.

## Where they look / how they vet
- **Where:** **warm intros and investor-network referrals** dominate; reverse-engineering products they admire and asking for the designer.
- **Vets by:** portfolio read for *decision-making narrative* and scrappy WIP (not glossy case studies); a **30-day test** ("one month tells me if you understand our product and can talk to our engineers"); the "tell me about a time a client pushed back on a design decision" question.

## Messaging that LANDS vs that REPELS
- **Lands:** "**One accountable senior partner — product, site, and brand.**" · "You talk to the person doing the work — no account managers, no junior swap." · "**Embedded continuity without agency overhead or a premature exec hire.**" · "I scale with you and help you build the in-house team when it's time." · A clear **bus-factor answer** (documented files, backup on call, clean handover).
- **Repels:** the word "freelancer"; cheap/$ pricing that undercuts the senior frame; anything implying a junior team or middlemen; "I'll do whatever you ask" order-taker energy. Sell *partner, continuity, accountability* — and price like a real engagement.
