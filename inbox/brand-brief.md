# Brand Brief — Personal Site for Yev (Senior Product Designer)

> Source: provided by client (Yevhen "Yev" Ledenov). Verbatim.

**Core positioning:** Yev is the senior design hire a founder needs before they can afford a design team. Not a freelancer who makes things pretty, not an agency with account managers and overhead. One accountable partner who handles product, site, and brand with senior judgment, at founder speed. Working positioning line (refine in copy, don't hardcode): "Senior product design at founder speed. One partner for product, site, and brand, before you can afford a team." Every section reinforces "one senior partner" over "I do many things."

**Who the client is:** Seed–Series B founders, but the real target is their state, not their funding stage. Time-poor, runway-conscious. Already burned once: by a cheap Upwork designer (pretty files, no product logic), by an agency (slow, expensive, middlemen), or by DIY AI tools like v0/Lovable (generic output). Too early for a full-time design hire, too serious to ship something half-baked.

**What Yev is chosen against (the positioning beats all four at once):** vs junior — senior product judgment, not just execution. vs agency — no middlemen, direct line, faster. vs DIY-AI — AI-accelerated but senior-directed, not generic. vs early full-time hire — get senior output now, commit to a team later.

**The runway nerve (central emotional driver):** the fear to speak to is "I'll pay for Figma files that never become a real product, and the process will eat my runway." This drives the tone. Problem-first, diagnostic. Talk about the founder's risk, not the designer's features. Production-ready output that ships is the antidote.

**Why breadth is a feature, not a liability:** Yev covers product UX/UI, websites, copy, brand, AI-tooling. Frame this NOT as "I can do lots of things" (jack-of-all-trades) but as "one accountable partner instead of coordinating three vendors." Breadth equals value only through the single-ownership frame.

**Services (group under the single-partner frame):** product UX/UI (dashboards, web apps, mobile incl. iOS and RTL/Arabic); product and marketing sites (landing to multi-page, copywriting included); design systems, prototyping, micro-animation (Figma Smart Animate, Lottie); AI-assisted design workflows (embeds Claude/Midjourney/v0/Cursor, builds custom tooling like design-audit pipelines and prompt generators for client projects); front-end-aware design (hands off work that drops cleanly into React/Next.js/Tailwind/Webflow).

**Stack:** Figma, Webflow, React/Next.js/Tailwind, AI tooling (Claude, Midjourney, v0, Cursor). Designs with implementation in mind, the bridge between design and dev.

**Proof and credibility:** enterprise logos (Google, Nestlé, P&G, AB InBev, WhatsApp) used SMALL as a trust signal, never the hero. Frame as "I've seen how the best operate and bring that rigor to your stage," not "I worked at big companies." Avoid the dissonance of "why is someone WhatsApp-level freelancing my landing page." Credentials: 11 international design awards, Dribbble 18M+ all-time views, Upwork Top Rated Plus 10+ years, Human-Centered Design degree (SWPS University).

**Portfolio (build as a scalable data structure, not hardcoded):** project card with tags (industry, type, year) plus a detailed case-study page.
- Flagship/long-standing: Vuelo (FCA-regulated fintech), Formulae Prescott (Marie Claire UK Awards 2023), Clubforce (803K-member platform), HostIQ.ai (SaaS for short-term rental hosts, Linear aesthetic, desktop + mobile), Burai (operational intelligence), Kinder Dispo (US real estate wholesaling), Confyde (clinical trial platform), Halyk Bank, Coursinity, Wrk, Scout Travel, Chladni.
- Recent (shows current range): The Animals/Huddl (culture & competitive-intelligence dashboard, dual human/machine visual language), GridSpace (venue platform, isometric SVG illustration), Nader (telehealth for Saudi market, Arabic RTL), Star Doctors (LatAm medical booking with social layer), Pastry (AI drama streaming + brand identity).

**Identity and voice:** persona, not studio — the site is "Yev" the person, founders buy a specific senior human, not a "studio-of-one." Keep Ledo OÜ as the legal entity in the footer only. Tone: sharp, diagnostic, problem-first. No marketing fluff, no "I'm passionate about design," no feature-dumping. Short sentences. Confidence without bragging. Write about the client's problem, not Yev's features. Language: site is in English (international audience). Copy rule: no decoratively-used em-dashes, use commas/parentheses, keep em-dashes only where genuinely needed.

**Conversion:** primary CTA is book a call (Calendly connected), secondary is direct message. Don't route the primary CTA to Upwork, keep the senior direct-partner frame.

**Aesthetic direction:** restrained product minimalism with one strong accent reads most clearly as "senior." Yev's work spans Linear-minimalism (HostIQ), isometric (GridSpace), and editorial duality (Huddl); the SITE itself must pick ONE direction or it loses focus. Default to the minimalist lane unless directed otherwise.

**Business details (footer/legal):** legal entity Ledo OÜ (Estonia), based in Zagreb Croatia, brand domain ledo.digital, model freelance/part-time with multiple parallel clients, languages Russian/Ukrainian/English (fluent).
