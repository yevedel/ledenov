02

The two closest analogues — study first

Simon McCade — the template to beat

Dark theme, single blue accent, work-forward. The cleanest expression of Yev's own pitch.

  • Eyebrow: PRODUCT DESIGN FOR B2B SAAS — instant relevance filter.
  • Headline (problem-first): "Let's fix your confusing user experience."
  • Subhead (outcome + proof): "I've helped 30+ B2B SaaS teams turn unfriendly products into intuitive experiences…"
  • Dual CTA: Book discovery call (primary) + Read case studies (secondary).
  • Inline proof: founder avatars + "Trusted by 30+ software founders" under the CTA.
  • Hero visual: real product screenshots, not illustration.

For Yev: borrow the eyebrow → problem-headline → outcome-subhead → dual-CTA → inline-proof stack. Differentiator: broader single-partner scope + the heavier $2M / 950 contracts proof.

Oli Batstone — proof framing + scarcity

Light theme, centered hero. Best example of the enterprise-logo framing the brief prescribes.

  • Scarcity bar: "Availability for 2 new clients. Book a call →" — urgency without hype.
  • Eyebrow → problem headline: health-startups eyebrow → "You've built it. People just don't engage."
  • Logo framing: "With 10 years of experience, I've worked with these brands" (Uber, Google…) — kept quiet. Exactly the framing for Yev's Google/Nestlé/P&G/WhatsApp logos.
  • Next section: "Typical problems I solve" — problem-led, not a feature list.

For Yev: adopt the "worked with these brands" label, the scarcity bar, and the "problems I solve" services framing.

03

Solo / productized tier — pricing & offer mechanics
  • Designjoy — canonical solo operator. Flat $4,995/mo shown openly, "Pause or cancel anytime," nav includes See pricing. Louder/more colorful than Yev's lane — borrow the structure, not the look.
  • Artone Studio — Designjoy clone for startups, "One monthly fee. No contracts," $4,000/mo.
  • UI Flip — strongest solo-vs-agency copy: "Solo by design. Direct line, no agency layers." Problem headline "You shipped without a designer. Now it's costing you growth." $6,000/mo.
  • btsta — design-engineer angle ("Product design that ships," "embedded partner, not an agency"), scarcity ("one slot open").
  • SaaS Designer / UXIsaac — outcome-led heroes ("booked demos"), low-friction CTAs ("Book 15min Call" / "Send Project Brief").
Pricing pattern (research-backed): ~10/11 high-performing productized sites show pricing openly; rule of thumb — if avg deal < $10K/mo, show the price. Decision for Yev: /pricing with packages — show numbers or "starting at"?

Aesthetic refs (craft only): Rauno Freiberg & Paco Coursey — reference-grade Linear/Vercel minimalism, no funnel. Study for typography, whitespace, motion polish; don't copy their no-CTA structure.

04

Cross-cutting patterns — what converts

Hero

  • Eyebrow label = instant "is this for me?" filter (PRODUCT DESIGN FOR [SEGMENT]).
  • Headline = the founder's problem as a short sentence, not the service.
  • Subhead = who you help + outcome + a proof number.
  • Dual CTA: Book call (primary) + case studies / project brief (secondary).
  • Inline proof under the CTA (avatars + "trusted by N founders").
  • Hero visual = real product screenshots, never decorative illustration.

CTA

  • Multiple placements: above fold, after each case, dedicated contact. Vary wording by context.
  • Action + duration beats generic: "Book a 20-minute intro call" > "Contact me". After a case: "Want results like this?"
  • Tasteful scarcity ("availability for 2 clients", "one slot open").

Proof

  • Logos framed as experience/rigor ("worked with these brands"), kept small — never the hero.
  • Outcome-based testimonials with real numbers, placed inline (inside cases / next to services).
  • Avatar clusters + "trusted by N founders."

Case studies

  • 3–5 deep cases beat 10 shallow ones; narrative, not a thumbnail wall.
  • Arc: Hook (outcome in one line) → Client context → Problem (specific, with metrics) → Process & decisions → Results (quantified).
  • Before/after is often the single most persuasive image for a founder.
  • Top portfolios lead with business impact / result first, then process.

Mistakes to avoid

  • Portfolio-as-art-gallery (polish over sales mechanics).
  • Vague taglines / "I work with all kinds of businesses" (kills specialist signal).
  • Services as feature lists instead of "problems I solve."
  • Screenshots without problem→process→results.
  • Ignoring mobile.

05

Gap & opportunity for Ledenov

No competitor combines elite Linear-grade minimalist craft (Rauno/Paco) with a tight book-a-call founder funnel (Simon/Oli). Craft sites have no funnel; funnel sites look templated. Yev's site can own that intersection — with $2M / 950-contract proof heavier than any analogue's "30+ teams," and a single-partner scope (product + site + brand) broader than the SaaS-only specialists.

Concrete moves to borrow (mapped to our IA)

  1. Hero — add an eyebrow (PRODUCT DESIGN FOR FOUNDERS); problem-first headline; subhead = outcome + Upwork proof; dual CTA; inline avatars/"trusted by". — Simon McCade
  2. Scarcity bar — optional top strip ("Booking N projects this quarter"). — Oli Batstone
  3. Logos — label "Brands I've brought senior rigor to / 10+ years," keep small. — Oli Batstone (+ NDA guardrail)
  4. Services → reframe as "Problems I solve," problem-led not feature-led. — Oli Batstone
  5. Case studies → 3–5 deep, Hook→Context→Problem→Process→Results, before/after, quantified. — Alex Berman / DesignerUp
  6. Pricing → /pricing with packages; decide show-number vs "starting at". — productized tier
  7. Motion / craft bar → hold to Rauno/Paco restraint so the site reads "senior," not "template." — aesthetic refs
