# FAQ — client questions & answers (research-grounded)

> Built from deep-research on what clients weigh when hiring a designer/agency + the questions/objections they actually raise. ⚠️ Research hit a session usage limit mid-run, so adversarial verification was incomplete — but the question set is standard and well-documented; answers are grounded in Yev's site facts.
> Voice: sharp, direct, problem-first. Order: highest-intent / highest-anxiety first (price, who-does-the-work, ownership, risk). Use a subset on the homepage/pricing FAQ; the full set can live on a dedicated page.

## What clients actually weigh (decision factors, ranked)
1. **Who actually does the work** (fear: junior bait-and-switch) — the #1 agency anxiety; a solo senior wins here.
2. **Relevant experience / does he get my domain** — portfolio relevance + industry fit.
3. **Process & research evidence** — not just pretty screens; how decisions are made.
4. **Transparent pricing & what's included** — vague pricing = instant rule-out.
5. **Ownership (files/IP) + developer handoff** — do I own it, will it ship, does he stay available.
6. **Communication & reliability** — cadence, responsiveness, no ghosting.
7. **Speed / availability / startup-pace fit.**
8. **References / proof it worked** — reviews, repeat clients.

## Top objections to pre-empt
- "Pretty Figma files that never ship" · scope creep / hidden costs · junior bait-and-switch · lock-in · ghosting / slow replies · "doesn't know my industry."

## The FAQ (Q → A, grounded in Yev)

### Engagement & pricing
**What does it cost?**
Projects start at $500. Ongoing work is $1,000/week. We scope the exact number together on a call, so you commit knowing it — no surprise invoices.

**How do you price, and what's included?**
Per-project or a weekly rate, whichever fits. Either way you get production-ready design (Figma), front-end-aware handoff, copy where it's needed, and a direct line to me. Open pricing, scoped before we start.

**Do you do one-off projects or ongoing work?**
Both. A defined project, or an embedded weekly partnership for product, site, and brand. Most clients start with one thing and come back, several do a 5th and 10th project with me.

**What if it's not working out? How do we part ways?**
No lock-in. Ongoing work pauses or stops anytime. We also pressure-test fit on the first call, so we don't start something that isn't right.

### Who & how
**Who will I actually work with, you or a junior?**
Me. Always. I'm a solo senior designer, so the person you talk to is the person doing the work. No account managers, no hand-off to juniors.

**How do we communicate, and how often?**
A direct line, call, plus Slack/WhatsApp for the day-to-day. I reply within hours and show progress early and often, not one big reveal at the end.

**What's your process?**
Strategy → UX → UI → delivery, senior decisions first, in fast cycles. Every step ties back to what we're trying to ship. (See "How it works" on the site.)

**How fast can you start, and how long will it take?**
I usually start within a week. A focused scope ships from about two weeks; bigger builds take longer. We set the timeline on the call so you're not guessing.

**How many revisions do I get?**
We iterate within the agreed scope until it's right. Senior decisions up front mean fewer "back to the drawing board" rounds.

**What do you need from me?**
Context on the product and users, access to what exists, and one decision-maker to react quickly. I keep the lift on you light.

### Trust & proof
**Do you have experience in my industry?**
Likely. 10+ years across fintech, AI, healthcare, B2B SaaS, Web3, ecommerce, and more, 950+ projects. See the work and the industry pages for niche-specific examples.

**Can I see relevant work and references?**
Yes, the work and reviews are on the site. I'm Upwork Top Rated Plus with 950+ projects and ~$2M delivered, and the strongest signal: clients come back (one is 5 contracts in since 2016).

**Are you actually senior?**
10+ years, 950+ projects, ~$2M delivered, Top Rated Plus. I bring that judgment to early-stage products without the agency overhead.

### Deliverables, ownership & handoff
**What do you actually deliver?**
Production-ready design in Figma, a front-end-aware handoff (React/Next.js/Tailwind/Webflow), copy where needed, and the reasoning behind each decision.

**Do I own the files and IP?**
Yes. You own everything, full source files and rights. It's your product.

**Do you also build it, or just hand off?**
I design with what ships in mind, so the work drops cleanly into your stack, and I stay available during implementation for questions. I don't disappear at hand-off.

**Do you sign an NDA?**
Always. Your product and data stay yours.

### Logistics & fit
**What time zone are you in?**
Based in Zagreb (CET). I work across US, EU, and Australia time zones.

**What languages do you work in?**
Work is in English. (Russian and Ukrainian fluent.)

**Why you over an agency or a cheaper freelancer?**
One accountable senior partner instead of middlemen or pretty-but-unshippable files. Senior judgment, founder speed, you talk to the person doing the work. (See "Why me" on the site.)
